01 · The Buyer

Who is actually
reading this page?

A procurement lead, electrical engineer, or project manager at a data center operator, utility, or industrial contractor. They arrived with a specific project in mind.

They already know what a padmount transformer is. They're not learning — they're evaluating Giga specifically against alternatives they already have on a shortlist.

High purchase intent Long eval cycle Multi-stakeholder sign-off
Buyer Mindset Map
What they need to answer immediately
Can Giga hit my kV/kVA range? Do they have inventory or custom options? Can they deliver on my timeline?
What builds confidence to act
Named customer logos, testimonials from similar buyers, US manufacturing, compliance certifications, clear specs.
The conversion blocker
They will not submit a form if they can't answer "can Giga actually build what I need?" within the first scroll. Every word above the fold must serve this question.
What they don't care about
Brand storytelling, company founding narrative, or any content that isn't directly relevant to their spec requirements and procurement timeline.
02 · The Page's One Job
The single conversion goal

Get a qualified procurement buyer
to click "Build a Quote."

Not educate. Not entertain. Not build brand affinity. One action — from a buyer who already has a project in mind. Every section either earns that click or gets removed. There is no neutral content on a conversion page.

Answer the spec question first
Up to 46 kV & 10,000 kVA. In-stock and custom. Hero headline leads with this — not brand, not story.
Earn trust before asking
Social proof (logos, testimonials from similar buyers), US-based in-house manufacturing, certifications — all arrive before the primary CTA.
Low-friction entry point
The build uses a quote request form as a prototype of this CTA. In production, "Build a Quote" becomes a step-through configurator — kVA range → voltage class → timeline → submit — reducing commitment anxiety versus a cold contact form. The form captures identical data and is a functional stand-in for this mockup.

04 · What Got Cut

Content judgment
under constraints

The copy pack had more material than the page needs. Every cut was made by asking: does a procurement buyer need this to feel confident requesting a quote?

If the answer was no, or if it duplicated a stronger element already on the page, it was removed. Noise reduces conversion.

Cut decisions
✕  Company origin story
The buyer is sourcing equipment, not evaluating a vendor relationship. Founding narrative has zero purchase-decision relevance for this audience.
✕  Bitcoin mining product mentions
On a padmount page targeting data center operators, bitcoin mining context erodes trust. Wrong signal for the audience.
✕  Blog / resource links
Exit links on a landing page bleed conversion. Resources belong in nurture, not on the quote-request path.
✓  What stayed: the spec range
46 kV / 10,000 kVA / 3-phase in the hero. This is the primary qualifier — the buyer needs to see it instantly to know they're in the right place.
05 · Measuring Success

What good looks
like at 30/60/90

This is a high-intent B2B page. Volume will be low, deal size is high. Optimizing for qualified quote request rate matters more than raw conversion rate. One mis-qualified lead is noise; one true SQL is significant pipeline.

GA4 — quote_request event, scroll depth, time on page

HubSpot — form submissions, lead source attribution, SQL conversion rate from page

Hotjar / MS Clarity — scroll heatmaps, click maps, session recordings on drop-off

06 · First A/B Test

The first test after 30 days of traffic

With a month of data, the first question is whether the hero is qualifying or confusing the buyer. The headline is the highest-leverage element — it determines whether the rest of the page gets read at all.

Control — A
Spec-led headline:
"Custom 3-Phase Padmount Transformers. Up to 46 kV & 10,000 kVA. In-stock and made-to-order."
Technical Qualification-first
Variant — B
Speed + spec headline:
"The transformer your project needs, built and delivered faster. Up to 46 kV & 10,000 kVA, US-made."
Outcome-led Urgency signal
Hypothesis: Because procurement buyers are often under timeline pressure, a headline that leads with delivery speed and US manufacturing alongside the spec claim will increase quote request rate versus a pure spec headline — measured by click-through to the quote builder over a 4-week window at statistical significance (p < 0.05, minimum 200 sessions per variant).
Implementation
Cloudflare Worker URL split — no Webflow plugin required. Variant assignment via cookie for consistent experience. GTM fires variant ID to GA4 on load.
Primary metric
Click-through rate on "Build a Quote" CTA. Secondary: time on page and scroll depth to spec section.
If B wins
Next test: subheadline — does adding "7-week delivery" as a proof point lift conversion further, or does it create skepticism?
07 · Funnel & Distribution

How this page connects
to the broader funnel

A landing page without a traffic and nurture strategy is a tree falling in an empty forest. This page needs three connected systems to generate sustained pipeline.

Paid traffic: LinkedIn Sponsored Content targeting data center procurement and electrical engineering job titles. Google Search on commercial-intent queries: "padmount transformer quote," "3-phase transformer manufacturer USA."

SEO: Long-tail product pages targeting spec queries ("46 kV padmount transformer," "10000 kVA 3-phase custom"). This page as primary target for transactional intent terms.

Retargeting: LinkedIn Insight Tag + Google remarketing for visitors who hit the page but don't convert. 7-day window, spec-specific ad creative.

Non-converters: What happens next
Immediate — Retargeting ads
Any visitor who scrolls >50% but doesn't convert enters a LinkedIn/Google retargeting audience. Ad creative mirrors the spec range they viewed. 7-day window before frequency cap.
Form submitted, not ready to buy
HubSpot workflow enrolls in a 4-email technical nurture sequence: spec guide → manufacturing explainer → customer case study → direct "ready to quote?" outreach from AE.
SEO long game
This page targets "padmount transformer manufacturer" and "custom 3-phase transformer USA." Supporting blog content ("how to spec a padmount transformer") feeds internal links to this page, building topical authority over 6–12 months.
Attribution
UTM persistence script stores first-touch campaign data in localStorage → populates HubSpot hidden fields on form submit → W-shaped attribution in HubSpot reporting credits first touch, lead creation, and opportunity creation.
08 · AI & Tooling in the Build

How AI accelerated this work — specifically

AI wasn't used as a novelty. It was used as an operating system for speed and quality. Here's exactly where it entered the workflow.

Site audit before building: Before writing a single line of Webflow, I audited gigaenergy.com's source code with Claude — identifying the WebFont.js render-block, the broken initSwiper() bug, the missing UTM persistence layer, and dual analytics fragmentation. This informed every build decision.

Copy drafting and refinement: Initial headline variants generated in Claude with a specific buyer-psychographics prompt, then manually refined. 3 variants tested in team review before committing to the build. Time saved: ~40 minutes of blank-page copy drafting.

Schema markup generation: Organization, Product, and FAQPage schema blocks generated and validated against schema.org spec with AI assistance. Dropped into Webflow Project Settings head. Ensures AI search systems (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Claude) can confidently cite Giga's specs.

UTM persistence script: The full first-touch UTM capture → localStorage → HubSpot hidden fields script was written with AI pair-programming. Tested against multi-page journey scenarios before deployment. Directly fixes the attribution gap identified in the audit.

A/B test hypothesis writing: Hypothesis format (Because we observed X, we believe changing Y will result in Z, measured by W) was drafted in Claude using actual page scroll data patterns from comparable B2B industrial pages. Ensures the test is designed to answer a real question, not just generate data.

llms.txt for AI discoverability: A structured llms.txt file was drafted and will be deployed via Cloudflare Worker so AI crawlers receive a structured company brief before parsing marketing-voice content. Positions Giga for citation in AI-mediated searches like "who builds AI data center infrastructure fastest."
